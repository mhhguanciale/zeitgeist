Top News

  • Middle East Escalation: US military forces bombed military targets on Iran's Kharg Island, a critical oil hub. Iran has threatened immediate retaliation against global oil facilities and shipping lanes.
  • Fed Leadership Crisis: A federal judge quashed DOJ subpoenas in the ongoing criminal probe of Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell, complicating the administration's efforts to reshape the central bank.
  • Tech Layoffs: Meta is reportedly planning a sweeping 20% workforce reduction to offset mounting AI infrastructure costs.
  • Domestic Energy Pivot: The administration invoked emergency powers to restart offshore oil drilling along the California coast to counter Middle East supply shocks.
  • Domestic Security Threats: A wave of domestic terrorism and violence has struck the US, including a vehicle attack on a Michigan synagogue, a shooting at a Texas ICE facility, and an attack at Old Dominion University.
  • Apple's 50th Anniversary: Apple kicked off its 50th-anniversary events, unveiling the highly repairable MacBook Neo.

Macroeconomic Outlook

  • Growth & Labor Market: The US labor market is showing definitive signs of cooling. February unemployment ticked up to 4.4% (from 4.1% last summer), and job openings have dipped below 7 million. Despite this, industrial production remains relatively resilient, preventing an immediate recessionary contraction.
  • Inflation & Rates: Core inflation remains sticky. While recent data revisions provided slight relief, the 10-year Treasury yield has surged to 4.27% in mid-March, steepening the yield curve (10Y-2Y spread at +0.55). The Fed Funds rate sits at 3.64%, but long-duration bonds are pricing in persistent inflation premiums due to supply chain and energy shocks.
  • Central Bank Leadership: Extreme uncertainty surrounds the Federal Reserve. With Chair Powell facing unprecedented legal and political pressure, markets are aggressively pricing in a leadership change, with Kevin Warsh viewed as the highly probable successor. Expect this transition to inject severe volatility into rate expectations and the US Dollar, which recently spiked to multi-year highs.

Geopolitics & Commodities

  • Middle East & Energy: The direct US/Israel conflict with Iran has fundamentally altered the energy landscape. Following the strikes on Kharg Island, WTI crude skyrocketed from $65 in late February to over $94 in early March. Expect sustained elevated oil prices and heightened risk for global shipping through the Strait of Hormuz. Overweighting domestic energy producers and defense contractors is highly recommended.
  • Russia-Ukraine: The conflict is evolving into a proxy testing ground, with the US now leveraging Ukrainian drone expertise against Iran. There is a growing market consensus pricing in a moderate probability of a ceasefire or frozen conflict by year-end, which could eventually ease European energy constraints, though near-term defense spending remains robust.
  • US-China Relations: Trade tensions remain a focal point, with expectations of aggressive blanket tariffs and specific tech-export restrictions. Supply chain decoupling will continue to benefit domestic industrials and near-shoring beneficiaries.

Sector Themes

  • Technology & AI: The AI capex cycle is facing its first major profitability test. Meta's planned 20% workforce cut highlights the unsustainable nature of current AI infrastructure costs without commensurate revenue growth. Competition remains fierce among OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google. Expect a rotation toward AI hardware and semiconductor providers over software until monetization improves. Apple's 50th anniversary and new hardware launches provide a defensive, consumer-oriented tech alternative.
  • Defense & Aerospace: Heightened global conflict and rising domestic security threats are driving sustained defense spending. The anticipated SpaceX IPO later this year is dominating aerospace discussions and is expected to set a record valuation, drawing massive liquidity into the space and defense tech sectors.
  • Cryptocurrency: Digital assets remain highly bid, with strong market expectations for Bitcoin to breach new all-time highs this year. Institutional adoption, favorable regulatory shifts, and the proliferation of stablecoins are supporting the broader crypto ecosystem as a hedge against fiat volatility and geopolitical instability.
