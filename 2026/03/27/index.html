Macroeconomic Outlook

  • Sticky Inflation & Rate Path: Disinflation has stalled. Core CPI continues to grind higher (reaching 333.5 in February), and Core PCE remains elevated. While the Fed Funds rate sits at 3.64% following cuts in late 2025, the market is aggressively pricing out further easing for 2026. Expect a "higher for longer" regime to persist through the year.
  • Yield Curve Steepening: The 10Y-2Y Treasury spread has un-inverted and steepened to +0.49%, with the 10-year yield hovering around 4.33%. Rising term premiums reflect entrenched inflation and deficit concerns. Avoid long-duration bonds; favor short-term paper and inflation-protected securities.
  • Labor Market Cooling: The unemployment rate ticked up to 4.4% in February, and JOLTS job openings have trended downward since late 2025. This divergence—cooling labor alongside sticky inflation—elevates near-term stagflation risks.

Overweight energy producers and direct commodity exposure.

    • Maintain exposure to AI infrastructure and hardware, but monitor the shifting competitive landscape.
    • Anthropic’s successful injunction against the Pentagon ban preserves its federal contracting viability, keeping the foundational model race highly competitive against OpenAI and Google.

Alternative Assets

  • Bitcoin & Crypto: Bitcoin is consolidating with high volatility. Corporate adoption continues (e.g., MicroStrategy's ongoing accumulation), and the regulatory environment remains broadly favorable. However, sticky macro rates provide a near-term headwind against explosive upside.
  • Gold: Maintain overweight. Gold serves as the optimal hedge against the dual threats of Middle East escalation and persistent fiat inflation.
