Daily Memo (12-Mar-2026)

Top News Headlines

  • Middle East Escalation: US and Israel's war with Iran intensifies; six tankers attacked in the Persian Gulf and Strait of Hormuz over two days.
  • Energy Shock: Brent crude breaches $100 a barrel amid Hormuz transit fears, rendering planned US Strategic Petroleum Reserve releases ineffective.
  • Northern Front: Hezbollah overwhelms Israel's Iron Dome with over 100 rockets, expanding the conflict into Lebanon.
  • Tech & AI: Atlassian cuts 10% of its workforce (1,600 jobs) to aggressively pivot toward AI development.
  • Hardware Releases: Apple launches new M5 Pro and M5 Max MacBooks, signaling continued silicon advancements.

Macroeconomic Outlook

  • Stagflationary Energy Shock: The rapid escalation in the Middle East has severely disrupted shipping. WTI crude has spiked violently from $65 in late February to nearly $95 by early March, with Brent crossing $100. This will immediately bleed into headline inflation and reverse recent disinflationary progress.
  • Monetary Policy Pivot: The Fed has steadily cut the funds rate down to 3.64% as of February. However, with Core CPI already ticking up to 333.5 last month and the new oil shock materializing, the Fed is highly likely to pause rate cuts. The 10-year Treasury yield is already pricing this in, climbing from 3.97% in late February to 4.15%. Expect continued upward pressure on the long end of the curve.
  • Labor Market & Consumer Health: The US consumer is highly vulnerable. Unemployment ticked up to 4.4% in February. The combination of surging pump prices, sticky healthcare costs, and rising borrowing costs will severely compress discretionary spending.
  • Geopolitical Risk Premium: The US Dollar is catching a strong safe-haven bid, with the Trade-Weighted USD Index rising from 117.7 to 119.4 in recent weeks. Expect sustained strength in the USD and Gold as the Iran conflict shows no signs of a near-term ceasefire.

Sector Strategy

  • Overweight Energy & Defense: Upstream oil producers and oilfield services are prime beneficiaries of the Hormuz disruptions. Defense contractors will see sustained backlog growth as the US and Israel actively engage Iran and Hezbollah, requiring heavy munitions replenishment.
  • Underweight Airlines & Consumer Discretionary: Jet fuel spikes are actively destroying airline hedging strategies, which will crush operating margins. Retail and hospitality are highly exposed to the tapped-out consumer reallocating budgets to basic energy and healthcare needs.
  • Mega-Cap Tech & AI: Large-cap tech remains insulated from the commodity shock and benefits from strong balance sheets. Enterprise AI disruption is accelerating, evidenced by Atlassian's workforce restructuring. Expect continued capital flows into AI infrastructure and semiconductor names as new models (e.g., GPT-5.5, Claude updates) are slated for release this year.
  • Alternatives: Gold remains a mandatory geopolitical hedge. Bitcoin is showing resilience and is heavily favored by market participants to reach new all-time highs this year, acting as a parallel hedge against fiat debasement and geopolitical instability.
  • Private Markets / IPOs: Market sentiment strongly anticipates a mega-cap SpaceX IPO later this year. AI-native companies (OpenAI, Anthropic) are also seeing massive private market valuation markups, which will continue to drive M&A activity in the software sector.
