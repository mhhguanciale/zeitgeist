Top News

Macroeconomic Outlook

  • Stagflationary Pressures: Inflation remains sticky, with February Core CPI rising to 333.5 and Core PCE trending upward. The current oil shock—with WTI crude spiking into the $90s—will bleed into headline CPI and transportation costs. Second-order effects from the fertilizer shortage will likely drive up Food CPI in the coming months.
  • Monetary Policy & Yields: Kevin Warsh is heavily favored to be confirmed as the next Fed Chair. Given the inflationary backdrop and a resilient labor market (unemployment at 4.4%), expect the Fed to pause any rate cuts. The 10-year Treasury yield has climbed to 4.42%, and the yield curve has un-inverted (10Y-2Y spread at +0.56). Expect a "higher for longer" rate environment to persist.
  • Fiscal & Trade Drag: The DHS shutdown is expected to drag on well past 40 days. Combined with the looming implementation of a 10% blanket tariff and targeted China tariffs, expect a measurable drag on Q1 and Q2 GDP growth.

Geopolitics & Commodities

  • Middle East Escalation: The U.S. and Israel are deeply engaged in military action against Iran and its proxies. Expect continued strikes and severe disruptions to shipping through the Strait of Hormuz and Bab el-Mandeb.
  • Commodity Supercycle:
    • Oil: Crude is heavily bid, with expectations of sustaining prices well over $100/bbl as Iranian and Gulf supply chains are threatened.
    • Gold: Acting as the ultimate safe haven, gold is experiencing massive inflows and is expected to shatter the $4,000/oz mark as sovereign and institutional buyers hedge against geopolitical tail risks.
    • Natural Gas: Domestic natural gas demand will surge, driven not just by traditional needs but by the sudden pivot of Big Tech to secure independent gas-fired power for AI data centers.
  • China-Taiwan: Short-term invasion risks remain low, but trade tensions are exceptionally high. Expect aggressive tariff posturing rather than kinetic military action in the near term.

Sectors & Equities

  • Defense & Aerospace: The rapid depletion of U.S. munitions (specifically Tomahawks) guarantees massive replenishment contracts. Defense primes are positioned for significant outperformance. Furthermore, the highly anticipated SpaceX IPO later this year will serve as a massive liquidity event and valuation benchmark for the broader aerospace sector.
  • Technology & AI: The AI model race is accelerating with the imminent releases of GPT-5.5 and Claude 4.7. However, the primary bottleneck has shifted from compute to power generation. Meta's procurement of gas plants signals a structural shift; infrastructure, utilities, and natural gas providers are now direct AI plays.
  • Cryptocurrency: Bitcoin is showing extreme resilience, pushing toward $70k with strong momentum to breach $80k. It is trading as a parallel safe-haven asset alongside gold. Expect continued aggressive corporate treasury purchases, led by MicroStrategy.
  • Airlines & Travel: The sector faces a perfect storm of headwinds: spiking jet fuel costs, TSA staffing strains from the DHS shutdown, and emerging pandemic variant risks ("Cicada" strain). Expect margin compression and lowered forward guidance.
