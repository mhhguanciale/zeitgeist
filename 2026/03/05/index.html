Investment Thesis: War Inflation & The "Higher for Longer" Reality

Macro Outlook: The Stagflationary Risk The convergence of escalating conflict in the Middle East and sticky domestic inflation data suggests the "soft landing" narrative is at risk. With the 10Y Treasury yield holding above 4.0% and the 2Y at 3.51%, the yield curve remains inverted but is steepening.

  • Rate Path: Markets have effectively priced out a March rate cut (<2% probability). The base case is now shifting toward a hold through Q2 2026. War-induced energy inflation will likely force the Fed to remain hawkish, capping upside for rate-sensitive sectors (Utilities, REITs) despite the AI power narrative.
  • China Deflation vs. War Inflation: While China's sub-5% growth target is deflationary for industrial metals (Copper, Iron Ore), this is being overridden by the supply-shock risk in Energy.

Geopolitics: Maximum Pressure on Iran The market is currently underwriting a high probability (>80%) of Iran closing the Strait of Hormuz by June 30. This is the single largest tail risk to the portfolio.

  • Second Order Effect: A closure would send Brent/WTI significantly higher (markets pricing >$90/bbl potential in Q2).
  • Third Order Effect: Global trade disruption affects supply chains, renewing inflationary pressure on goods, forcing central banks to delay easing cycles.
