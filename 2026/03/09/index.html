Markets are heavily pricing in the closure of the Strait of Hormuz and further strikes on Iranian infrastructure (Kharg Island, Fordow).

The 10-year Treasury yield has steepened to 4.13%, with the 10Y–2Y spread widening to +0.59. Expect a hawkish pause at next week's FOMC meeting.

  • Washington Gridlock: Trump’s legislative blockade over the SAVE Act guarantees prolonged government funding disruptions. The ongoing DHS shutdown is likely to extend, adding a negative fiscal impulse to an already fragile macro environment.

Sectors & Equities

  • Energy & Defense (Overweight): Prime beneficiaries of the current geopolitical environment. Domestic E&P companies and oil majors will capture the massive supply premium. Defense contractors are seeing structural tailwinds, with expectations of the US federal government taking direct stakes in key defense and aerospace firms to secure supply chains.
  • Consumer Discretionary & Retail (Underweight): The $110+ oil shock acts as a massive regressive tax on the consumer. Retail sales have already been stagnating (733k in Jan vs 734k in Dec), and February unemployment ticked up to 4.4%. Expect severe margin compression for airlines, travel, and retail.
  • Mega-Cap Tech & AI (Neutral): Nvidia's upcoming GTC is a major near-term catalyst; expect a heavy focus on "Blackwell" architecture, data center scaling, and AI agents. Apple's foldable iPhone leaks provide a 2026 hardware supercycle narrative. However, broad tech faces severe headwinds from rising 10-year yields and potential supply chain disruptions if the Middle East conflict drags on or spills into Asia.

Alternatives

  • Precious Metals: Gold is catching a massive safe-haven bid. With stagflation risks rising and geopolitical stability deteriorating, market consensus is pricing in a run toward $5,000+ per ounce by year-end. Silver is exhibiting high-beta leverage to the gold move.
  • Cryptocurrency: Bitcoin remains highly volatile, consolidating in the $60k–$70k range. Despite the risk-off macro environment, institutional ETF flows and sovereign adoption narratives (e.g., US strategic reserve discussions) are providing a firm floor.
